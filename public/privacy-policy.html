2. Definitions

2.1 Key Terms

  • Personal Information: Information that directly identifies or can reasonably be linked to identify an individual, including names, email addresses, phone numbers, postal addresses, identification numbers, and online identifiers.
  • Sensitive Personal Information: A category of personal information that warrants heightened protection, including financial information (bank account numbers, payment card details, income), biometric information (voice recordings, voice prints), social security numbers, health information, and precise location data.
  • Processing: Any operation performed on personal information, including collection, recording, organization, storage, alteration, retrieval, consultation, transmission, erasure, or destruction.
  • Controller: The entity (RKV Consulting LLC) that determines the purposes and means of processing personal information.
  • Processor: Any entity that processes personal information on behalf of the Controller (including Anthropic, Twilio, Supabase, Deepgram, ElevenLabs, Stripe, and SerpAPI).
  • Data Subject: The individual to whom personal information relates (you, your tenants, your employees, and your vendors).

7. Voice Recording & Transcription Processing

The Meridian Platform automatically records all voice communications, including tenant calls, landlord calls, and internal communications. This section describes how we capture, process, and retain voice data.

7.1 Automatic Call Recording

  • All inbound calls to Meridian numbers are recorded in full
  • All outbound calls from the Platform are recorded in full
  • Recordings include the complete audio stream, background noise, and all speech
  • Call recordings are stored in encrypted format in our Supabase database (hosted on AWS)
  • Metadata (caller ID, call date/time, duration, party identifiers) is indexed for retrieval

7.2 Consent & Legal Requirements

Two-Party Consent States: The following states require consent from all parties to record a call: California, Delaware, Florida, Illinois, Maryland, Michigan, Montana, New Hampshire, Pennsylvania, and Washington. If you operate in these states, you are responsible for obtaining consent from all call participants before calls are recorded through Meridian. This may require:

  • Verbal notification to callers that the call will be recorded
  • Obtaining explicit consent from all parties
  • Providing an automated announcement ("This call may be recorded")
  • Maintaining consent records for audit purposes

One-Party Consent States: In all other states, only one party (you) needs to consent to recording. However, we recommend informing all participants that calls are being recorded for quality and compliance purposes.

Responsibility: You agree that you are responsible for obtaining all legally required consents from tenants, vendors, employees, and other call participants. Meridian provides the recording capability, but compliance with consent laws is your responsibility. Violations of consent laws may result in significant legal liability for you.

7.3 Speech-to-Text Processing via Deepgram

  • Call recordings are automatically sent to Deepgram (a third-party speech recognition service) for transcription
  • Deepgram converts audio to text using machine learning models
  • Deepgram processes recordings through their API and may store them temporarily for processing
  • Completed transcripts are returned to the Meridian Platform within minutes
  • Transcripts are stored in our database alongside original recordings
  • Deepgram's privacy practices are described at https://deepgram.com/privacy

7.4 Text-to-Speech Processing via ElevenLabs

  • When AI generates responses to tenant inquiries, text is converted to speech using ElevenLabs
  • ElevenLabs processes text through their voice synthesis API
  • ElevenLabs generates natural-sounding voice output
  • Audio files are returned to Meridian for playback to tenants
  • ElevenLabs' privacy practices are described at https://elevenlabs.io/privacy

7.5 Biometric Data & Voice Prints

Important: Call recordings and voice transcriptions may constitute biometric data under state laws, particularly the Illinois Biometric Information Privacy Act (BIPA), Texas Capture or Use of Biometric Identifier (CUBI), and Washington state biometric laws. Voice prints created during transcription and text-to-speech processing are unique identifiers derived from an individual's voice characteristics.

We treat voice data with heightened privacy protection as required by these laws. This includes maintaining reasonable security, limiting access to authorized personnel, and providing notice and opt-out mechanisms for biometric collection.

7.6 Storage & Retention of Call Data

  • Call recordings are retained for 2 years from the date of the call
  • Transcripts are retained for 2 years
  • Metadata is retained for 2 years
  • After 2 years, recordings and transcripts are automatically deleted
  • You may request manual deletion of specific calls at any time
  • Backup copies may be retained for up to 30 days after primary deletion for disaster recovery

7.7 Opt-Out of Call Recording

You can disable automatic call recording for your account by contacting us at kishanramnath@rkv-consulting.com. However, disabling call recording may limit the Platform's functionality and your ability to generate transcripts and use AI-powered call analysis features. Some features may not work without call recordings.

7.8 Access to Recordings & Transcripts

  • You can access your call recordings and transcripts through your account dashboard
  • Recordings are searchable by date, caller ID, and keyword
  • You can download recordings for backup or compliance purposes
  • Transcripts are editable to correct transcription errors
  • You can share recordings with team members through the Platform

11. Data Retention & Deletion Policies

We retain personal information for different periods depending on data type and legal requirements. Below is our comprehensive retention schedule:

11.1 Retention Schedule by Data Type

  • Active Account Data: Retained while your account is active. Upon account deactivation, core account identifiers are retained for 90 days to allow data export and recovery. After 90 days, account data is deleted unless legal obligations require retention.
  • Call Recordings: Retained for 2 years from date of call. Automatic deletion occurs after 2 years unless you explicitly request longer retention for compliance purposes.
  • Call Transcripts: Retained for 2 years from date of call, synchronized with recording retention.
  • SMS Messages: Retained for 1 year from date of message. Automatic deletion occurs after 1 year.
  • Financial Records: Retained for 7 years to comply with tax reporting requirements (federal income tax, depreciation schedules, rental expense documentation).
  • Property Records: Retained for the duration of your account plus 5 years. Historical property data is useful for future sales and due diligence.
  • Tenant Records: Retained for 3 years after a tenant vacates to comply with fair housing documentation requirements and tenant screening industry standards. After 3 years, tenant contact information is deleted but aggregate screening decision data may be retained anonymously.
  • Usage Analytics: Raw event-level analytics data is retained for 1 year. Aggregated, anonymized analytics are retained indefinitely for trend analysis.
  • Support & Communication Logs: Retained for 2 years unless related to an active dispute or investigation, then retained until resolution plus 1 year.
  • Backup Copies: Maintained for 30 days after primary data deletion for disaster recovery purposes. Backups are then destroyed.
  • Legal Holds: Data subject to litigation, audits, or government investigations is retained until legal matters are resolved, regardless of standard retention periods.

11.2 Post-Account Termination

When you terminate your account:

  • You have 90 days to export your data in a portable format
  • After 90 days, we begin deletion of your account data
  • Financial records are retained for 7 years for tax compliance
  • Personal information about tenants is deleted after 3 years per industry standards
  • Call recordings older than 2 years are already deleted
  • Data may be retained longer if required by law or if you have outstanding disputes

11.3 Right to Request Deletion

You can request deletion of specific information at any time, subject to legal and contractual retention requirements. Requests should be sent to kishanramnath@rkv-consulting.com. We will comply with deletion requests within 30 days unless we have a legal obligation to retain the information.

11.4 Specific Deletion Requests

Certain data types have specific deletion mechanics:

  • Call Recordings: You can delete individual recordings or all recordings associated with a tenant or property. Deletion is permanent.
  • Tenant Records: Deletion of a tenant record removes their contact information and screening data but retains anonymized payment and lease history.
  • Property Records: Deletion of a property removes it from your active properties list but retains historical data for 5 years.

11.5 Deletion Delays

Data deletion may not occur immediately due to technical factors:

  • Cached data on CDNs may take up to 24 hours to clear
  • Backup systems may retain data for up to 30 days
  • Some data may be anonymized rather than fully deleted to maintain analytics integrity

12. Data Security Measures

We implement comprehensive technical, administrative, and physical security measures appropriate to the sensitivity of the data we process.

12.1 Encryption

  • In Transit: All data transmitted between your client and our servers is encrypted using TLS 1.2+ with AES-256 cipher suites. API calls to third-party services use HTTPS with certificate pinning where possible.
  • At Rest: Sensitive data (financial records, tenant PII, call recordings) is encrypted using AES-256 encryption at the database level. Encryption keys are managed separately from data to prevent unauthorized decryption.
  • Encryption Key Management: Encryption keys are stored in a secure key management service (KMS) with restricted access. Keys are rotated regularly per security best practices.

12.2 Access Controls

  • Role-Based Access Control (RBAC): Users have roles (Owner, Manager, Tenant, Viewer) with corresponding permissions. Only authorized personnel can access sensitive data types.
  • Principle of Least Privilege: Team members are granted only the minimum access required to perform their job functions.
  • Access Logging: All access to sensitive information is logged, including who accessed what data, when, and from what IP address.
  • Regular Access Reviews: We periodically review user access rights and remove unnecessary permissions.

12.3 Authentication & Session Management

  • Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA): Optional MFA is available using authenticator apps or SMS codes. We encourage all users to enable MFA, especially administrators.
  • Password Requirements: Strong passwords (minimum 12 characters, complexity requirements) are enforced.
  • Session Timeout: User sessions automatically expire after 30 minutes of inactivity for security.
  • Secure Password Storage: Passwords are hashed using bcrypt with salting. Raw passwords are never stored or logged.
  • SSO Integration: Single sign-on (SSO) via SAML 2.0 is available for enterprise customers for centralized identity management.

12.4 Network Security

  • Firewalls: Web Application Firewalls (WAF) protect against common attacks (SQL injection, XSS, DDoS).
  • Intrusion Detection: Intrusion detection systems (IDS) monitor for suspicious network activity.
  • DDoS Protection: Vercel provides automatic DDoS mitigation.
  • Network Segmentation: Internal services are segmented with restricted inter-service communication.

12.5 Application Security

  • Secure Code Review: All code changes are reviewed before deployment.
  • Dependency Management: Third-party libraries are kept up to date with security patches applied promptly.
  • Vulnerability Scanning: Automated security scanning identifies potential vulnerabilities before deployment.
  • Penetration Testing: Regular penetration tests by external security firms assess attack resistance.
  • Static Analysis: Code is scanned for security issues before production deployment.

12.6 Incident Response

  • Incident Response Plan: We maintain a documented incident response procedure.
  • Monitoring & Alerts: Systems are monitored 24/7 for security incidents and performance issues.
  • Breach Notification: In case of a data breach, we notify affected individuals and regulatory authorities within legally required timeframes (see Section 22).
  • Forensic Analysis: Security incidents are investigated thoroughly to determine root cause and prevent recurrence.
  • Post-Incident Review: After incidents, we review and improve our security practices.

12.7 Third-Party Security

  • Vendor Assessment: Subprocessors are evaluated for security practices before being used.
  • SOC 2 Compliance: Key subprocessors (Twilio, Stripe, SerpAPI, Deepgram) maintain SOC 2 Type II or equivalent compliance.
  • Regular Audits: We periodically audit subprocessor security practices.
  • Data Processing Agreements: Contracts with subprocessors include security and breach notification requirements.

12.8 Limitations of Security

While we implement comprehensive security measures, no system is completely impenetrable. We cannot guarantee absolute security against all attacks, breaches, or data loss. Potential risks include:

  • Zero-day vulnerabilities that are not yet known or patched
  • Sophisticated targeted attacks by well-resourced adversaries
  • Insider threats from compromised employees or contractors
  • Supply chain attacks affecting our subprocessors
  • User error (weak passwords, phishing attacks)

13.1 Illinois Biometric Information Privacy Act (BIPA)

If you operate in Illinois or have Illinois residents using the Platform, the Illinois BIPA (740 ILCS 14/1 et seq.) applies to voice data.

  • Scope: BIPA protects "biometric information," defined as information based on an individual's unique biological characteristics, including voice prints and voice patterns.
  • Notice Requirement: We provide this Privacy Policy which describes collection and use of voice data as required notice.
  • Consent Requirement: BIPA requires written consent before collecting biometric information. By using Meridian for voice calls, you consent to voice recording and biometric processing. For tenants, you must obtain consent before recording their calls.
  • Retention Limitation: We retain voice data for 2 years, which is reasonable for business purposes (legal compliance, dispute resolution, quality assurance).

14. Automated Decision-Making & Profiling

The Meridian Platform uses AI and automated processes to make decisions that may significantly affect you, your tenants, and your business. This section describes automated decision-making, profiling, and your rights regarding such processing.

14.1 Types of Automated Decision-Making

Tenant Screening Scores

Meridian's AI analyzes tenant applications to generate a 0-100 tenant qualification score based on:

  • Credit score and credit history
  • Employment verification and income stability
  • Criminal background (relevant to Fair Housing Act standards)
  • Rental history and eviction records
  • Application completeness and consistency

The score is advisory only. You retain full discretion to approve or reject tenants regardless of score. The score should not be the sole factor in tenant decisions.

Fair Housing Compliance Flags

AI analyzes your communications, decisions, and practices for potential Fair Housing Act violations, including:

  • Discriminatory language in rejection letters or communications
  • Disparate treatment based on protected characteristics (race, color, national origin, religion, sex, disability, familial status)
  • Disparate impact of policies on protected groups
  • Service animal and disability accommodation issues

Flags are informational and do not create legal liability. You are responsible for ensuring Fair Housing Act compliance.

Investment Deal Scores

AI generates investment recommendation scores (0-100) for properties based on:

  • Property valuation (Zestimate-style estimates)
  • Rental income potential
  • Expense ratios (maintenance, vacancy)
  • Market growth projections
  • Cash flow and ROI estimates

These scores are predictions based on historical data and are not guarantees of future performance. Past performance does not ensure future results.

Maintenance Prioritization

AI may recommend maintenance work prioritization based on:

  • Urgency (tenant safety, tenant satisfaction)
  • Condition assessment from photos or reports
  • Vendor availability and cost estimates
  • Seasonal factors

Rent Setting Recommendations

AI can recommend rent prices based on comparable properties (comps) from SerpAPI, adjusted for property-specific factors. Recommendations use market data and statistical analysis but do not account for individual tenant circumstances.

14.2 Profiling

The Platform creates profiles of you as a landlord/investor and of your tenants based on behavioral data. Landlord profiles may include:

  • Preferred property types and locations
  • Average tenant qualification standards
  • Maintenance responsiveness patterns
  • Communication preferences
  • Feature usage patterns

These profiles are used to personalize your experience and make relevant recommendations. You can request deletion of your profile at any time.

14.3 Significant Automated Decisions

A "significant" automated decision is one that produces legal or similarly significant effects. In the property management context, significant decisions include:

  • Tenant approval/rejection recommendations with scores below 30 or above 70
  • Fair Housing compliance red flags
  • Large financial impact recommendations (e.g., major expense recommendations, rent increase suggestions)

For significant decisions, we aim to provide:

  • Clear notification that a decision is automated
  • Explanation of the key factors that influenced the decision
  • Information on how to contest the decision
